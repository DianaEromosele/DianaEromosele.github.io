Let's unpack this abstract that none of us can live without.

Diana Ozemebhoya Eromosele | April 2016

Here are a few things about this phenomenon we call "the Internet" that I culled from this cool YouTube video.

1. The Internet is a wire buried in the group. What's it made of? Fiber optics, optics and it can beamed to satellties or through cellphone networks.

2. Two computers connected to the Internet can communicate. That's why we can e-mail, text-message and FaceTime.

3. In fact, a Server is a computer that is connected directly to the Internet. So most of the personal laptops and computers that we ordinary people use are not servers. They're called Clients.

4.Webpages are files on a server's harddrive.

5. Every server has a unique IP address. The user-friendly version of an IP address is the domain address, like Google.com, or Facebook.com, or YourPersonalBlog.com.

6. Here's the connection flow of your client laptop: 

   Personal Laptop (the Client) -- Internet Service Provider (Optimum, DSL) - Internet (The wire in the ground)
7. You can then look at webpages and communicate with machines that are connected to the Internet directly (servers) and indirectly (other clients like yourself).

8. **Everything** connected directly or indirectly to the Internet has an IP address. Not just servers. Clients, cell phones, smartphones, routers, etc.

9. That's how machines communicate and are able to send data to each other, using the IP addresses of every machine connected to the Internet.

10. A Router is a piece of equipment that lay at the virtual intersection of where two or more parts of the Internet connect. Routers help transport data around the Internet.
